Numark Mixtrack Pro FX vs Pioneer DDJ-200: Which Entry Controller Wins?

Both controllers cost around $149–$179 and target absolute beginners. But they take completely different approaches — and picking the wrong one will frustrate you for months. Here's the honest comparison.

Build Quality

The Numark Mixtrack Pro FX feels denser. Its jog wheels are larger (6 inches vs Pioneer's 4.3 inches), which makes manual beatmatching easier to learn. The metal faceplate and weighted faders actually feel like a proper club mixer in miniature. The DDJ-200 is lighter, more plastic-feeling — designed for portability, not club simulation.

Software Ecosystem

This is where they diverge sharply. The Numark works with Serato DJ Lite (upgradeable to Serato DJ Pro). Serato is the industry standard at clubs, bars, and events. If you plan to play out, learning Serato now is smart. The DDJ-200 works with rekordbox, WeDJ, djay Pro, and edjing Mix. The mobile compatibility is unique — it's the only sub-$200 controller that connects wirelessly to a phone.

When to Pick the Mixtrack Pro FX

Choose the Numark if: you're learning at home on a laptop, you want the closest simulation of club gear, or you plan to upgrade to Serato DJ Pro eventually.

When to Pick the DDJ-200

Choose the Pioneer if: you want to DJ from your iPhone or iPad, travel frequently, or plan to stay in the Pioneer/rekordbox ecosystem long-term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What software is compatible with the Numark Mixtrack Pro FX?

A: The Numark Mixtrack Pro FX is compatible with Serato DJ, DJ Pro 6, and Rekordbox DJ, giving you a range of software options to choose from.

Q: Can I use the Pioneer DDJ-200 with Rekordbox DJ on a Windows computer?

A: Yes, you can use the Pioneer DDJ-200 with Rekordbox DJ on a Windows computer, but you will need to use the WeDJ app on your iOS or Android device to control the DDJ-200.

Q: Is the Numark Mixtrack Pro FX a good choice for beginners?

A: While the Numark Mixtrack Pro FX is a versatile controller, it may be a bit overwhelming for beginners due to its feature-rich design and steeper learning curve. It's recommended for beginners to start with the Pioneer DDJ-200 and then upgrade to the Mixtrack Pro FX once they have more experience.

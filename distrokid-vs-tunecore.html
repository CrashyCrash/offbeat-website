DistroKid vs TuneCore vs RouteNote: Which Pays Artists More? (2026)

In the ever-evolving music industry, independent artists face numerous choices for distributing their sound. Among the leading players, DistroKid, TuneCore, and RouteNote have emerged as popular platforms that cater to the distribution, monetization, and promotion of music. However, for artists focused on maximizing their earnings from streams and sales, the choice of distributor can make a significant difference. In this article, we’ll break down the core pricing structures, features, and payout models of DistroKid, TuneCore, and RouteNote in 2026.

Overview of Music Distribution Platforms

DistroKid

DistroKid, known for its user-friendly interface and quick delivery times, charges a flat annual subscription fee starting at $24.99 per year for unlimited uploads. Artists keep 100% of their royalties, which makes it an attractive option for those seeking simplicity in fee management. With fast payouts and streamlined services, DistroKid serves as a reliable choice for smaller-scale artists looking for no-frills solutions.

TuneCore

TuneCore offers a more tailored approach to distribution, catering to artists at different levels of their careers. The pricing structure involves flat rates per release, with album uploads starting at $29.99 annually and singles costing $9.99 annually. TuneCore boasts a feature-rich environment for artists looking to manage their careers professionally, offering premium add-ons like publishing administration. Importantly, TuneCore also allows its clients to keep 100% of their revenue, much like DistroKid.

RouteNote

RouteNote offers a unique model that primarily appeals to emerging artists or those with budgetary constraints. Users can choose between a free plan, which keeps 85% of revenue (RouteNote takes a 15% cut), and the premium plan, where artists retain 100% royalties by paying a fee upfront per release. The platform features customizable revenue-sharing options and minimal payout thresholds starting at $50.

Payouts Comparison

DistroKid Earnings in 2026

DistroKid continues to prioritize fast payouts, reporting earnings within three to five days after a release goes live. The platform has no commission, allowing artists to receive 100% of their royalties. However, there’s a minimum payout threshold of $20, making it ideal for regular earners but less suited for those generating smaller income installments.

TuneCore Earnings in 2026

TuneCore has shown remarkable financial performance, reaching the milestone of $5 billion in artist payouts since its inception. Artists enjoy monthly reports, with no commission charges, and can keep 100% of their revenue, even from social platforms. This makes TuneCore a robust option for those dabbling in multi-channel revenue streams.

RouteNote Earnings in 2026

RouteNote caters to beginners and budget-conscious artists by offering a free plan (keeping 15% commission) and a premium plan for 100% royalty retention. While beneficial for entry-level distribution, the platform’s $50 payout threshold and monthly cycle may delay smaller earnings. Artists also benefit from expansive revenue-sharing tools for collaborative projects.

How to Choose Between DistroKid, TuneCore, and RouteNote

When selecting a music distributor, it’s essential to consider your career stage, budget, and earning potential. Here’s how the platforms measure up:

Best for Beginners

RouteNote provides a free plan that lets novice artists test the waters of digital distribution without upfront costs. Though the 85% royalty retention may seem less attractive, the lack of upfront fees makes it a viable option.

Best for Intermediate Artists

DistroKid’s flat pricing model is great for artists releasing multiple singles or albums annually. With 100% royalty retention and faster payouts, artists can keep more of their earnings.

Best for Professionals

TuneCore is ideal for seasoned musicians aiming to make significant income from their craft. With high transparency, solid track record, and no commission charges, TuneCore offers support for publishing, promotion, and monetization across multiple channels.

Final Verdict

For artists prioritizing no-commission models and keeping their income intact, TuneCore stands out as the premium choice, particularly for those earning from diverse revenue streams. DistroKid, with its simplicity and speed, is best for indie musicians dropping regular releases. In contrast, RouteNote holds appeal for emerging artists looking to minimize costs upfront.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DistroKid or TuneCore better for music distribution?

DistroKid wins for artists releasing frequently: unlimited releases for $22.99/year, regardless of how many singles or albums you put out. TuneCore charges per release ($9.99/single, $29.99/album) but lets you keep 100% of royalties with no annual subscription. If you release 3+ singles per year, DistroKid is cheaper.

Does TuneCore take a percentage of streaming royalties?

No. TuneCore keeps 0% of your streaming royalties — you receive 100% of earnings from Spotify, Apple Music, and all other platforms. You pay a flat annual fee ($9.99/single/year, $29.99/album/year) instead. Previous TuneCore pricing tiers have been updated; verify current pricing at their website before committing.

How fast does DistroKid distribute music to Spotify?

DistroKid typically delivers to Spotify within 1–3 business days. Using the 'Deliver to stores immediately' setting in DistroKid can reduce this to same-day or next-day for pre-verified accounts. Spotify's processing on their end adds an additional 1–3 days for the track to become searchable.

Can I use both DistroKid and TuneCore at the same time?

No — you cannot distribute the same song through multiple distributors simultaneously. Duplicate submissions cause store takedowns and royalty disputes. Choose one distributor per release. You can migrate existing releases from TuneCore to DistroKid, but take down the old version only after the new one is fully live.

What is DistroKid's 'Leave a Legacy' feature?

Leave a Legacy ($29/year add-on) keeps your music live on streaming platforms even if you stop paying for your DistroKid subscription. Without it, music is removed when you cancel. This is useful for artists who want passive income from older releases without paying full subscription fees indefinitely. Activate it before canceling if you want music to remain permanent.

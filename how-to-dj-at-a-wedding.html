How to DJ at a Wedding: Complete Guide for 2026

<p>Wedding DJing pays $500–$2,500 per event and has a lower technical bar than club DJing. It also has much higher stakes for failure — this is someone's one day. Here's how to prepare properly and get paid well.</p><h2>Equipment for Wedding DJs</h2><p>Minimum pro setup: two CDJs or a multi-channel controller + laptop, a powered PA system with sub (minimum 1,500W for a room of 100 guests), a wireless microphone for announcements, and backup audio on a phone. Recommended gear:</p><ul><li>Controller: Pioneer DDJ-FLX10 or similar 4-channel unit</li><li>PA: QSC K12.2 (×2) + QSC KS118 subwoofer</li><li>Wireless mic: Shure BLX24/SM58</li><li>Cables: always bring 2× spares of every cable you own</li></ul><h2>Timeline Management</h2><p>A standard 4-hour wedding reception has clear sections: cocktail hour (background jazz/acoustic), dinner (ambient/soft pop), first dance + family dances (pre-requested specific songs — DO NOT improvise these), cake cutting, open dancing (peak energy), and last dance (slow closer). Get the timeline in writing from the couple 2 weeks before.</p><h2>Handling Requests</h2><p>The hardest skill in wedding DJing: taking requests without losing dance floor momentum. Accept all requests, but stage them by energy level. Never say no directly. If someone requests a song that would kill the dance floor, say 'Absolutely, I'll work it into the set' and play it at the natural energy transition point — usually never.</p><h2>The Backup Plan</h2><p>Bring a second laptop with your full music library pre-loaded. Bring two power strips. Know where the venue's circuit breakers are. Know your venue manager's phone number. Professional wedding DJs earn their rate by never having a technical failure ruin a ceremony.</p>

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do wedding DJs charge?

Typical rates: $500–$1,000 for beginners (first 5 weddings), $1,000–$1,800 for experienced DJs, $2,000–$3,500+ for high-end wedding market DJs in major cities.

Do I need special insurance to DJ weddings?

Yes. Most venues require liability insurance for vendors. DJ liability insurance runs $180–$350/year through providers like DJ Insurance or Next Insurance.

Should I use streaming services or download all songs?

Always download and own your music. Streaming can fail at the worst moment. Never DJ from Spotify or Apple Music live.

How Our Affiliate Links Are Labeled

On every review and recommendation page, affiliate links are visually identified. You'll see a small label next to or near the link so you always know when a link is part of an affiliate program. Here's an example of what an affiliate link looks like in our HTML:

<a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/EXAMPLE?tag=offbeatdj-20" class="affiliate-link" rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank" data-affiliate="amazon"> Check Price on Amazon <span class="aff-label">(affiliate link)</span> </a>

Every affiliate link on this site uses rel="nofollow sponsored" to comply with search engine and FTC guidelines. The (affiliate link) label is visible to readers so there is never any ambiguity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are affiliate links labeled on Offbeat pages? +

Every product link that is part of an affiliate program carries a visible (affiliate link) label. In the HTML, these links also include rel="nofollow sponsored" so search engines and readers can clearly identify them. We never hide affiliate relationships.

Do prices change for readers who use affiliate links? +

No. The price you pay is exactly the same whether you click our affiliate link or go directly to the retailer's website. Our commission comes from the retailer at no additional cost to you. In some cases, using our link may even surface a coupon or deal that benefits you.

Can brands pay for positive reviews on Offbeat? +

Absolutely not. Brands cannot pay us for a positive review, a higher ranking, or inclusion in any of our "best of" or recommendation lists. If a brand sends us a product for evaluation, our assessment is honest and independent. Any product provided for review is disclosed in the article itself.

How are your recommendations chosen and updated? +

We research products based on specifications, professional and user reviews, price-to-value ratio, build quality, and real-world performance. Our team tests products wherever possible. Pages are reviewed and updated regularly — at least twice per year — to reflect price changes, new firmware, or newer competing products. Last-updated dates are displayed on every article page.

What happens if an affiliate link is broken or a product is discontinued? +

We run regular link audits to catch broken or redirected affiliate links. If a product has been discontinued, we update the page to either remove the link or replace it with a current alternative. You can always report a broken link via our contact page and we'll fix it promptly.

Do you use affiliate revenue to influence which products appear on the site? +

No. Affiliate revenue does not determine which products we feature or how we rank them. Our editorial process is independent of our monetization. We include affiliate links as a way to keep the site running and fund continued research — not as a factor in our recommendations.

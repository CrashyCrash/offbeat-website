Corrections & Fact Checks

We take accuracy seriously. If you spot an error in our testing data, product specs, pricing, or compatibility notes, send the details and we'll verify them against primary sources.

What helps us verify a correction fast

  • The exact page URL and section heading
  • What is currently listed and what you believe is correct
  • A link to an official source such as a manufacturer page, manual, firmware note, or press release
  • Your name or handle if you want to be credited for the correction

Preferred sources: brand product pages, manuals, firmware changelogs, or direct confirmation from a manufacturer representative. Retailer listings can help, but we treat them as secondary evidence.

Partnerships

We review brand partnerships case by case. Editorial independence comes first, so we only work with programs that fit a reader-first review site.

Good Fit Not a Fit
Transparent commercial partnerships with clear terms Paid inclusion or pay-to-rank offers
Loaner review units with no editorial approval rights Sponsored reviews presented as independent verdicts
Transparent educational partnerships with clear labeling Link exchanges or bulk guest-post outreach
Relevant DJ or music production brands with useful products Unrelated offers that do not help our readers

Include the partnership name, reader benefit, territories covered, and whether you can provide product documentation or review-unit logistics. Do not send pay-to-rank or link-exchange offers.

Press / Media

We can help with interview requests, commentary on DJ gear trends, beginner buying advice, and the practical side of home-studio or live-performance workflows.

Include these details in a press request

  • Your name, publication, and role
  • The topic, angle, or question set
  • Your deadline and preferred format
  • Whether you want product-specific context from pages such as our DJ controller guide or our beginner DJ software guide

Accessibility Feedback

If any page is hard to use with a keyboard, screen reader, magnification, or mobile browser, send us the details. Accessibility reports are treated as product issues, not as general feedback.

Details that make accessibility fixes faster

  • The page URL where the barrier appears
  • Your device, operating system, and browser
  • The assistive technology you used, if any
  • What you expected to happen and what actually happened

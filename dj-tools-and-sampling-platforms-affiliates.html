Which Program Should You Promote?

For sample pack and beat-making creators: Splice ($11.20/sale) and Airbit (25% recurring) are the strongest pairings. Splice is the dominant sample subscription platform — tutorials showing how to flip Splice samples convert well. Airbit's recurring commission model means one sign-up earns every billing cycle, making it unusually high-value for producers with loyal audiences.

For mastering and production workflow content: LANDR at $15 flat per sale has a verified 14.29% conversion rate, meaning roughly 1 in 7 clicks converts. If you cover mixing and mastering topics, LANDR is an easy embedded link. The 30-day cookie is standard and the direct affiliate program keeps the relationship straightforward.

For EDM and DJ crate-digging content: Beatport at 15% per sale stacks well with music discovery content. If you run "tracks of the week" roundups or DJ set analyses, Beatport affiliate links are a natural fit. The rumored +5% via Loopmasters stacking hasn't been confirmed as of 2026 — verify before relying on it.

DistroKid and BandLab are worth testing if your content is oriented toward independent artist distribution and release strategy. DistroKid doesn't publish its commission rate publicly — you'll need to apply and confirm before building content around it. BandLab's referral is a gift-card model rather than a true affiliate commission, so factor that into your expectations.

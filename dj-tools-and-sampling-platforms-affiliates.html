A MIDI controller is a device that sends MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) signals to computers or other hardware to control instruments, software, or effects. These controllers can feature keys, pads, knobs, and sliders, making music creation more intuitive, expressive, and efficient compared to using only a mouse and keyboard.

Should I choose a keyboard or pad MIDI controller for music production?

The choice depends on your workflow. Many producers opt for hybrid controllers with both keys and pads, offering versatility. Consider how you prefer to compose, the genres you work in, and whether you prioritize portability or full-sized keys for expressiveness.

In 2026, MIDI controllers often feature enhanced connectivity (Bluetooth, USB-C), customizable velocity sensitivity, integrated displays, and expanded DAW integration for seamless workflows.

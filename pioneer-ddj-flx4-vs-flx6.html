Pioneer DDJ-FLX4 vs DDJ-FLX6: Which Mid-Range Controller?

✏️ By Jake Morrison 📅 May 2026 ⏳ 8 min read
Disclosure: Offbeat Inc. earns commissions from qualifying purchases made through links on this page, at no extra cost to you. Learn more →
<p>Pioneer's FLX series is their current mid-range lineup. The FLX4 sits at ~$299, the FLX6-GT at ~$499. Both run Serato and rekordbox. Both have 16 performance pads per deck. So why pay $200 more? Here's exactly what you get — and when it's worth it.</p><h2>Key Differences</h2><p>The FLX6-GT adds a 4-channel mixer (the FLX4 is 2-channel), a dedicated booth output, and larger 6-inch jogwheels with capacitive touch sensitivity. The 4-channel mixer lets you mix from two separate sources simultaneously — useful for B2B sets or mixing CDJs with a laptop.</p><p>The FLX4 has 5-inch jogwheels, a 2-channel mixer, and no booth output. It's lighter and more portable. The build quality is similar — both use plastic construction on the jog platter housing.</p><h2>Performance Pads</h2><p>Both have 8 pads × 2 banks = 16 per deck. The FLX6-GT pads feel snappier with less pre-travel. The FLX4 pads are perfectly usable but require slightly more force for hot cue triggering.</p><h2>Who Needs the FLX6-GT</h2><p>Buy the FLX6-GT if: you play B2B sets (two DJs sharing one controller), you need to route microphone or instrument inputs through a booth monitor, or you want to future-proof with 4-channel mixing.</p>

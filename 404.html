Explore by Musician Type

Different DJs and producers have different needs, and Offbeat organizes content accordingly. If you're a bedroom DJ learning the fundamentals, start with beginner guides and budget equipment recommendations. If you're preparing for your first club gig, you'll want to understand professional controllers and mixing techniques. If you produce electronic music, check the music production section for DAW comparisons, plugin recommendations, and audio interface guides. Professional DJs will find advanced workflow guides, gear reviews for established platforms like Rekordbox and Serato, and technical deep dives. Knowing which musician type fits your goals helps you navigate faster.

Streaming DJs and broadcasters may need different equipment than club DJs — focus on reliable controllers, quality audio interfaces, and software that handles streaming protocols. Mobile DJs performing weddings and events need portable, battery-powered equipment — see the budget and wireless sections. Turntablists focused on scratching and mixing vinyl will want to explore pro turntable guides and mixer reviews. Each musician type has specific needs, and Offbeat's organization makes it easy to find recommendations tailored to your style. Use this framework to zero in on relevant guides within seconds.

Usually it’s a typo, an outdated bookmark, or a page we renamed when we updated a guide. Try the search box above, or pick a category that matches what you were looking for.

Was the page removed?

Sometimes older reviews get replaced by newer versions (for example, “Best DJ Controllers 2026”). If you were trying to find a specific model review, the Controllers hub is the fastest recovery path.

I’m brand new — what should I read first?

Start with the Beginner DJ Setup Guide, then pick either controllers under $200 (best value) or headphones under $100 (quick win).

Do you have a site map?

Not yet. Until we publish one, the “Popular pages” list above is the best shortcut to the major hubs.

How do I report an error on a guide?

Do you cover music production gear too?

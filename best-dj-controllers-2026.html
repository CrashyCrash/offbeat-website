Who Should Buy a MIDI Controller?

MIDI controllers are not just for professional DJs; they're also perfect for:

  • Beginners who want to start their DJing journey
  • Intermediate DJs looking to upgrade their setup
  • Producers and musicians who want to enhance their creative process
  • Live performers who need a reliable and portable instrument

Whether you're a seasoned DJ or just starting out, a MIDI controller is an excellent investment for any music enthusiast.

5 Key Features to Look for in a MIDI Controller

When shopping for a MIDI controller, keep the following key features in mind:

1. Number of Controls: A minimum of 16 pads, 8 sliders, and 8 knobs are essential for a basic setup. However, more advanced controllers can offer up to 64 pads, 16 sliders, and 16 knobs.

2. MIDI Protocol: Ensure the controller supports MIDI over USB, MIDI over Bluetooth, or both. This will give you flexibility when connecting to your computer or mobile device.

3. Compatibility: Check if the controller is compatible with your DAW (digital audio workstation) of choice. Most popular DAWs, such as Ableton Live, Traktor, and Serato, have native support for MIDI controllers.

4. Build Quality and Durability: A sturdy and well-built controller will withstand the rigors of live performances and frequent use.

5. Price: MIDI controllers can range from under $100 to over $1,000. Set a budget and balance it with the features you need.

Honest Pros and Cons of Popular MIDI Controllers

Here are some popular MIDI controllers and their pros and cons:

  • Native Instruments Traktor Kontrol S2: Excellent build quality, intuitive controls, and seamless integration with Traktor. However, it's pricey (around $400) and may not be compatible with other DAWs.
  • Novation Launchpad Pro: A versatile and feature-rich controller with a large pad layout. However, it can be overwhelming for beginners, and the build quality is not the best.
  • Akai MPK Mini MKII: A compact and portable controller with a great price-to-feature ratio. However, it may lack some advanced features and has a smaller pad layout.

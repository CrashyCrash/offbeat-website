Frequently Asked Questions

What headphone impedance is best for DJing?

DJ headphones typically range 32Ω–70Ω. Lower impedance (32Ω) works better with smartphone/controller outputs. Higher impedance (70Ω, like Sennheiser HD 25) needs more power but delivers louder, cleaner sound from professional DJ mixers. For most setups, 32–70Ω is the practical range.

Can I use studio headphones for DJing?

Studio headphones like Audio-Technica ATH-M50x work for bedroom practice. For club or event DJing, they have two problems: low max SPL (99 dB vs 120 dB club standard) and limited one-ear monitoring. DJ-specific headphones (HD 25, HDJ-X10) solve both issues.

Why do DJs wear headphones over one ear?

DJs monitor the next track in one ear while hearing the room/speakers in the other. This allows beat matching — aligning the incoming track's tempo with the playing track by ear before fading it in. One-cup swivel (180°) is a must-have feature for live DJing headphones.

Sennheiser HD 25 vs Pioneer HDJ-X10 — which is better?

The HD 25 ($149) is better for most DJs: lighter (140g vs 298g), replaceable components, and 120 dB SPL handles any venue. The HDJ-X10 ($299) wins on audio accuracy and frequency response — worth the premium for studio recording or high-end sound system environments.

What to Look for in Budget DJ Headphones

When shopping in the sub-$100 range, you have to prioritize. You cannot get everything, so focus on these three pillars:

In a DJ booth, your biggest enemy is "bleed"—the sound from the main speakers leaking into your headphones. Closed-back headphones are mandatory. Look for models with thick padding and a secure fit to ensure you can hear your cue track over the house music.

2. Driver Size and Response

Most DJ headphones utilize 40mm drivers, which is the industry standard for a balance of size and sound. Larger drivers (like the 50mm in the OneOdio) generally offer more bass, which is great for feeling the beat, but can sometimes mask the mid-range frequencies you need for precise beatmatching.

Budget headphones often fail at the cable. Check if the cable is detachable. If a cable frays or snaps, replacing a $10 cord is much better than replacing a $50 pair of headphones.

Frequently Asked Questions

What headphones do DJs use?

Professional DJs typically use closed-back, over-ear headphones designed for high sound pressure levels and maximum noise isolation. Industry standards include the Sennheiser HD 25 and Pioneer HDJ series, which prioritize durability and a punchy low-end to ensure the DJ can hear their cue tracks over loud club speakers.

Do I need closed-back headphones?

Yes, closed-back headphones are essential for DJing. Unlike open-back headphones, which let sound escape and enter the earcups, closed-back designs seal the audio in. This prevents the "bleed" from the main venue speakers from interfering with your ability to hear and beatmatch your next track accurately.

What impedance is best for DJs?

For most DJs, low impedance (typically 32 to 50 ohms) is best. Low-impedance headphones are easier to drive, meaning they can reach high volumes using the built-in amplifiers of standard DJ controllers or mixers without requiring an external headphone amp to sound full and loud.

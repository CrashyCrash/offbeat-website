Who I Am

I'm Jake Morrison. I started DJing at college in 2012 — not because I planned to, but because someone put a pair of CDJs in front of me at a campus party and nobody else knew how to use them. That was it.

By 2014 I was playing regular bookings in Chicago. By 2016 I was teaching beginner workshops because I kept getting the same questions: "What gear should I buy?" and "Which software is actually better — Serato or rekordbox?" The honest answer was always "it depends on how you play" — but nobody online was explaining why.

That gap is what this site is about. I test gear in real conditions — club gigs, home studio sessions, airport lounge DJ sets. I track specs against manufacturer data. I note what breaks, what lags, and what actually sounds right in a loud room at 1am.

Offbeat Inc. is my vehicle for turning that experience into structured guides. Some content is AI-assisted (DatBotty handles first drafts and research); everything is reviewed by me before it goes live.

My Review Methodology

Every guide on this site follows the same four-step process:

  1. Real conditions first. I test gear at actual gigs where possible, or in a home setup that mirrors live use — laptop on a table, mixer at arm's reach, headphones on hard for an hour.
  2. Spec verification. Every claim about latency, jog platter size, bit depth, or compatibility is cross-checked against the manufacturer's current data sheet — not a spec sheet from two years ago.
  3. Long-term notes. I keep notes on gear I've used for 6+ months. First impressions lie. The DDJ-400 feels great on day one and the pitch fader starts skipping by month four — that's the stuff that matters.
  4. No pay-to-play. Affiliate links are disclosed on every page. Nobody pays for placement. If a product scores poorly in testing, it says so — whether or not there's an affiliate program.

What Most Reviews Get Wrong

After reading hundreds of DJ gear reviews, a few patterns come up constantly:

Jog Platter Size ≠ Feel

A 6" jog can feel more responsive than an 8" one depending on firmware tension curves. Size is mentioned constantly; tension calibration almost never is.

USB Latency Under Load

Most latency specs are measured in lab conditions. Plug in a MIDI keyboard, a USB drive, and run a live recording simultaneously — the number changes.

Software Bundle Versions

"Includes Serato DJ Lite" means very different things in 2020 vs 2026. Always check which version is bundled and what's behind the upgrade paywall.

Licensing Costs Over Time

The controller is a one-time cost. The software subscription isn't. A $300 controller review that ignores $200/year in mandatory software costs is missing the point.

Amuse vs DistroKid vs CD Baby: Best Music Distribution for Indie Artists in 2026

✍️ By Jake Morrison 📅 Updated May 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read

ⓘ Disclosure: This page uses affiliate links. We may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. Full disclosure →

For the independent artist in 2026, the barrier to entry has never been lower, but the choice of distribution can make or break your financial trajectory. As streaming dominates and "waterfall" release strategies (dropping frequent singles) become the industry standard, selecting a partner that aligns with your output volume and budget is critical. Whether you are a bedroom producer looking for a $0 entry point, a prolific songwriter dropping a track every two weeks, or a legacy artist building a timeless catalog, the "big three"—Amuse, DistroKid, and CD Baby—offer radically different philosophies. This guide breaks down the pricing structures, free tier limitations, and royalty splits to help you decide where your music belongs in the current digital ecosystem.

Amuse: The Mobile-First Gateway and Free Tier

Amuse has solidified its position as the premier choice for emerging artists who are budget-conscious. Their "Amuse Start" free tier remains a powerful draw, allowing artists to distribute music to major platforms like Spotify and Apple Music without an upfront cost. However, the trade-off is speed and control; free users typically experience slower release windows and limited access to advanced analytics. For those ready to scale, the "Boost" subscription unlocks faster delivery and expanded store reach. Amuse's mobile-centric workflow is ideal for the Gen-Z creator who manages their entire career from a smartphone, though the lack of a robust desktop experience can be a hurdle for power users managing complex metadata.

DistroKid: The Engine for Prolific Creators

DistroKid operates on a high-volume subscription model that has fundamentally changed how indie artists release music. For a flat annual fee (typically starting around $22.99), artists get unlimited uploads. This makes it the undisputed champion for the "singles-first" strategy prevalent in 2026. Beyond just distribution, DistroKid provides essential growth tools like HyperFollow pages and automatic split payments, which allow collaborators to get paid their share of royalties automatically without the primary artist acting as a middleman. The primary risk is the subscription trap: if you stop paying your annual fee, your music may be removed unless you pay an additional "Leave a Legacy" fee per song.

CD Baby: The Catalog Builder's Choice

While the industry has shifted toward subscriptions, CD Baby remains the gold standard for those who prefer a "pay-once, keep-forever" model. Instead of an annual fee, CD Baby charges a one-time fee per single or album. This is an invaluable advantage for artists who release music infrequently or want to ensure their catalog remains live indefinitely without recurring bills. While the upfront cost is higher than a DistroKid subscription, the long-term cost of ownership is often lower for album-focused artists. CD Baby also offers superior administrative services, including professional publishing administration, making it a more comprehensive "business" partner than a simple delivery tool.

Pricing and Royalty Breakdown for 2026

The "100% royalties" claim is common, but the fine print varies. DistroKid and Amuse (on paid tiers) generally allow artists to keep 100% of their earnings. Amuse's free tier is also competitive, but they often leverage their platform to scout talent for their internal label. CD Baby traditionally took a small commission (around 9%), though they have evolved their pricing to be more competitive with subscription services. When calculating your ROI, consider not just the upload fee, but the "maintenance cost." A DistroKid user paying $23/year for a decade spends $230 to keep their music live, whereas a CD Baby user pays a single upfront fee and never pays again.

Quick Verdict

  • Choose Amuse if: You are just starting out, have a $0 budget, and prefer managing your music via a mobile app.
  • Choose DistroKid if: You release music frequently (1+ single per month) and want the fastest possible delivery to stores.
  • Choose CD Baby if: You release full albums, value long-term stability over low upfront costs, and don't want to worry about annual subscriptions.

The right distributor is the one that removes the friction between your studio and your listeners. If you're ready to get your music on the global stage, choose the platform that fits your current output and long-term goals. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Amuse or DistroKid better for independent artists?

DistroKid is better for most independent artists. It offers unlimited releases for $22.99/year, faster delivery (often 1–2 days), and stores royalties with 100% kept by you. Amuse's free tier works for testing distribution but its Pro plan ($59.99/year) is harder to justify against DistroKid's features at the same price point.

Does Amuse take a percentage of royalties?

Amuse's free plan keeps 100% of royalties for you. Their Pro plan also keeps 100%. However, Amuse has been known to delay payouts and has limited customer support response times. DistroKid and TuneCore both keep payouts more reliable with transparent reporting dashboards.

How long does music distribution take with DistroKid?

DistroKid typically delivers to major stores (Spotify, Apple Music) within 1–5 business days. Using their 'Deliver to stores immediately' setting reduces this to 1–2 days for most platforms. Spotify and Apple Music are usually live first; smaller platforms like Tidal and Deezer can take 7–14 days.

Can I switch from Amuse to DistroKid without losing streams?

Yes, but carefully. Use the 'Keep your existing URIs' option on your new distributor to preserve Spotify's internal track ID. Without this, playlists that feature your track will lose it. Take down the Amuse release only after the DistroKid version is fully live to avoid any gap in availability.

What happens to my music if I cancel DistroKid?

If you cancel DistroKid, your music will eventually be removed from all streaming platforms. DistroKid offers a 'Leave a Legacy' option ($29/year) to keep music live after you stop actively paying for a subscription. Always download your release metadata and streaming stats before canceling any distributor.

Amuse vs DistroKid vs CD Baby: Best Music Distribution for Indie Artists in 2026

Amuse vs DistroKid vs CD Baby 2026: free tier pricing, royalty splits, and which music distribution service is best for independent artists.

✍️ By Offbeat Editorial Team📅 Updated June 2026⏱️ 5 min read

Introduction

For the independent artist in 2026, the barrier to entry has never been lower, but the choice of distribution can make or break your financial trajectory.

Quick Verdict

  • Choose Amuse if you are just starting out, have a $0 budget, and prefer managing your music via a mobile app.
  • Choose DistroKid if you release music frequently and want the fastest possible delivery to stores.
  • Choose CD Baby if you release full albums, value long-term stability over low upfront costs, and do not want annual subscriptions.

Compared Options

Amuse

Amuse has solidified its position as the premier choice for emerging artists who are budget-conscious. The Amuse Start free tier lets artists distribute music to major platforms like Spotify and Apple Music without an upfront cost.

  • Best for beginners and mobile users
  • Free entry point
  • Paid tiers improve speed and flexibility

DistroKid

DistroKid operates on a high-volume subscription model that changed how indie artists release music. For a flat annual fee, artists get unlimited uploads.

  • Best for prolific singles artists
  • Fast delivery to stores
  • Works well for frequent release schedules

CD Baby

CD Baby remains the catalog builder's choice for artists who prefer a pay-once, keep-forever model instead of a recurring subscription.

  • Best for albums and legacy catalogs
  • One-time fee per release
  • Long-term stability over low upfront cost

Pricing and Royalty Tradeoffs

The common 100% royalties claim depends on the plan and provider. DistroKid and Amuse paid tiers generally let artists keep 100% of earnings, while CD Baby uses a release-based model that can appeal to catalog-focused artists.

Release Strategy Fit

Singles-heavy artists usually benefit from unlimited uploads. Album-focused artists may prefer a one-time fee model if they plan to keep releases available for years without managing annual renewals.

Bottom Line

DistroKid is the most practical default for frequent independent releases, Amuse is the lowest-friction starting point, and CD Baby is strongest for long-term catalog releases.

Practical checklist before you decide

Use this page as one part of the decision, not the whole decision. Confirm the current price, software compatibility, operating-system support, and whether the option still fits the way you actually practice or perform.

  • Fit: choose the option that matches your current workflow and the setup you expect to use for the next year.
  • Compatibility: verify exact hardware, app, subscription, and file-format requirements before buying or switching.
  • Reliability: avoid workflows that depend on one fragile adapter, one unstable app version, or an internet connection with no backup.
  • Upgrade path: favor tools that can grow with you instead of forcing another purchase as soon as you start recording mixes or playing longer sets.

How to use this guide in a real DJ setup

Before changing gear, software, or workflow, connect the recommendation to an actual use case: home practice, recorded mixes, streaming, mobile events, club preparation, or production crossover. A choice that looks best on paper can still be wrong if it adds setup friction or does not match the way you will play.

For practiceChoose the option that helps you build repeatable habits: organized libraries, clear cueing, reliable monitoring, and enough controls to practice without menu diving.
For recordingCheck recording support, local-file requirements, audio routing, export settings, and whether streamed tracks are restricted.
For gigsPrioritize reliability, backup options, wired connections, compatible outputs, and a setup that can survive a long set without updates, adapters, or internet access becoming the weak point.

The safest workflow is to test the setup exactly as you will use it, then document the cable path, software version, library source, and backup plan. That prevents most of the avoidable failures that happen when DJs buy the right-looking tool but never validate the whole system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Amuse or DistroKid better for independent artists?

DistroKid is better for most independent artists. It offers unlimited releases for $22.99/year, faster delivery (often 1–2 days), and stores royalties with 100% kept by you.

Does Amuse take a percentage of royalties?

Amuse's free plan keeps 100% of royalties for you. Their Pro plan also keeps 100%.

Is CD Baby better than DistroKid for albums?

CD Baby can be better for album artists who prefer a one-time release fee instead of keeping an annual subscription active.
